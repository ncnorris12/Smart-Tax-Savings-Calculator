About this free federal tax calculator (2025 & 2026)

The Smart Tax Savings Estimator is a free, browser-based federal income tax calculator that helps you estimate your tax refund, marginal tax bracket, and take-home pay for the 2025 and 2026 tax years. No sign-up, no email, no data collection — everything is computed in your browser.

Built on the IRS 2025 tax brackets and the 2026 brackets introduced under the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBB), this tax estimator supports all filing statuses: Single, Married Filing Jointly, Married Filing Separately, and Head of Household. Model your standard or itemized deduction, Qualified Business Income (QBI) deduction, self-employment tax, HSA and 401(k) contributions, Child Tax Credit, and long-term capital gains — all in one place.

What you can calculate

  • Federal income tax refund or balance due for tax years 2025 and 2026
  • Effective and marginal federal tax bracket
  • Take-home pay after federal tax, FICA, and self-employment tax
  • Tax savings from HSA, 401(k), Traditional IRA, and pre-tax contributions
  • QBI deduction for sole proprietors, LLCs, and S-corp pass-through income
  • Capital gains tax on long-term and qualified dividend income
  • Year-end tax planning moves personalized to your situation

2026 federal income tax brackets

The federal income tax has seven rates in 2026: 10%, 12%, 22%, 24%, 32%, 35%, and 37%. The 2026 standard deduction is $16,100 for single filers, $32,200 for married couples filing jointly, and $24,150 for head of household.

Frequently asked questions

Is this tax calculator free? Yes. The Smart Tax Savings Estimator is 100% free to use, with no ads on the calculator itself and no account required.

Is my data private? All calculations run locally in your browser. Your income, deductions, and other tax information never leave your device.

Does it include state taxes? No — this is a federal-only tax calculator. Always consult a qualified tax professional for state tax questions and for advice specific to your return.
